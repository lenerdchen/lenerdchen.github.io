How might we become market leaders in a crowded online privacy space?

Key Collaborators
Director of Product Design, Group Product Manager

My Contributions
User Research, Product Strategy, Concept testing

THE CHALLENGE

Define the product & brand differentiation between our stable of VPN services.

Our company recently acquired a stable of 3 VPN services. To grow these brands without cannibalising market share, we needed to establish product & brand differentiation by focusing on different user problems & segments.

My challenge was to discover the unmet needs of our users, define our user segments and product propositions, and align our leaders on the product strategy for each brand.

THE PROCESS

Understand our users deeply to define jobs-to-be-done desired outcomes.

I paired with a Senior UX Researcher to interview 24 users and survey thousands more to understand usersâ€™ concerns, attitudes, and behaviours in protecting their online privacy & security.

We defined three core user personas, and used visual storytelling to demonstrate to product managers & brand leaders the scenarios of their unmet needs & problems.

Concept testing through scenarios & stories to identify high potential opportunities.

I worked with 2 designers & a product manager to ideate on how we could potentially solve these user jobs, and develop lo-fi prototypes that told the story of these solutions.

Through user testing, we identified the problems & solution experiences that resonated with each user segment. We defined design principles and built interactive prototypes to showcase the product experience for each user.

THE RESULT

Visualising the experience enables alignment and long-term buy-in.

Everyone in the company had an opinion on what we should invest in next & how to grow each brand. I led multiple workshops with Product, Engineering, Marketing & Brand leaders to share our understanding of users, align their opinions against market & user insights, and build their ideas based on the key jobs to be done.

Through these workshops & user testing, we defined the unique value propositions & brand positioning for the 3 VPN brands, and identified 9 product opportunities to be further developed across the brands.
