User Research, Design Leadership

THE CHALLENGE

Simplify the end-of-life experience to make it more empathetic & less effortful.

When a loved one passes away, citizens have to transact across many government agencies and physical touch-points to register their death, manage funeral & burial services, and settle their estate.

My challenge was to lead 8 government agencies to redesign the end-of-life experience and develop a one-stop service to guide citizens through this difficult time.

THE PROCESS

Map the experience to highlight citizen pain points and back-end inefficiencies.

The end-of-life process was complex because government agencies were optimising their own parts of the process without looking at the experience from the citizenâ€™s perspective.

I led a team of 3 UX designers to interview citizens, end-of-life professionals, and front-line public service workers to map out the end-of-life service blueprint, highlighting major pain points, inefficiencies, and opportunity areas.

Two-pronged approach to simplify the process and resolve issues up-stream.

First, we needed to make post-death administration simpler & more empathetic for citizens. We collected instructions & content from all 8 public agencies, re-wrote them as step-by-step guides, and designed a single-stop resource to walk citizens through this difficult time.

Second, we also needed to encourage citizens to make & share plans with their families before death happens, so their loved ones feel confident & guided on their choices after they are gone. We built a vault where citizens could make healthcare, funerary & estate administration plans, and share them with trusted people to use after death.

THE RESULT

Building empathy for our citizens breaks down long-standing agency boundaries.

Through the discovery research & design sprint, we helped the public agencies see that each interaction caused friction & frustration for citizens at this difficult time.

Our design principle was to build data linkages and remove extra touch-points wherever possible. For example, we streamlined & digitised the death registration process, removing the burden from the family. In the vault, we automatically pulled in existing end-of-life plans, providing a complete view with no extra action needed.
