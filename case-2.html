How might we enable users to see & feel the value of an ‘invisible’ tool?

Key Collaborators
Product Manager, Product Designer

My Contributions
User Research, Prototyping, Product Design

THE CHALLENGE

Increase the number of paying users at the end of our 7-day free trial experience.

From our product data, we know that users who connect to the VPN more frequently are more likely to convert. However, there is a steep drop in engagement for new users after the first day, which led to eventual churn.

My challenge was to discover why these new users weren’t engaging with the VPN after their first day, and define experiments to increase the mean number of days connected.

THE PROCESS

Build a problem-solution hypothesis framework to structure our experiments.

I led interviews with 8 free trial users & surveyed 500+ more users, and identified the blockers that prevented them from engaging more with the VPN during the free trial.

We discovered that many first-time, casual users needed guidance to understand how to use the VPN, evaluate the quality of the service, and how to make the most of it.

I reframed these insights as problem hypotheses, and led brainstorming with the cross-functional team to develop solution ideas to tackle these user problems.

Lo-fi concept testing to identify most effective solutions.

Partnering another designer, we built interactive prototypes that demonstrated our solution hypotheses. I led user testing with 8 free trial users, evaluating their reactions to the updated onboarding & first use experiences.

We discovered that the users had an ‘a-ha’ moment of realising the VPN’s value when we showed them data of how the VPN was working. We redesigned the first connection experience, and launched an A/B test to see if it would increase their mean days connected and eventual conversion.

THE RESULT

Demonstrating the VPN’s performance & value increases engagement & retention.

The redesigned first connection experience led to a +3.7% increase in Day 8 conversion amongst free trial users.

The experiment gave us confidence that the problem hypothesis around users not understanding the VPN’s working & its value to their daily lives was worth pursuing further. We continued to experiment on other solution hypotheses to demonstrate the VPN’s performance & value (e.g., threats blocked, connection speed).
