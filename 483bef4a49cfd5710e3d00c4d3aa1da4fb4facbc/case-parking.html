How might we...
reduce the incidence of frivolous appeals against parking fines?


e-Service for Appeals against Parking Fines

We receive thousands of appeals against parking fines each year.

The majority of these appeals are rejected. However, when we went out to speak to drivers, we heard many say the opposite – that the majority appeals are accepted. This was probably because many successful appellants share their 'success' stories, while those whose appeals are rejected do not go around broadcasting their 'failures'.

When we spoke to the parking authorities, we realised it was not simply a problem of reducing the volume of appeals. We wanted to reduce the incidence of frivolous appeals, but not turn away those with legitimate reasons to appeal.

The problem was not just reducing the volume of appeals - but increasing the accuracy of appeals. We want to encourage appellants with legitimate causes to proceed, while those with frivolous cases should not waste their time and effort.
